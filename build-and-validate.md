# Build & validate — the implementation roadmap

**Purpose.** The design phase is closed (Dave, 2026-06-28): ~18 measurement branches are specified (Round 1's 10 + R1–R6 in [`measurement-avenues.md`](measurement-avenues.md)). This file is the **build loop's backlog** — the implementation analog of `measurement-avenues.md` — driving the re-aimed cron loop from *designing* branches to *building and validating* them. One branch per iteration, same disciplines, `make check` stays green.

## Current state (green baseline, 2026-06-28)
`make check` passes (exit 0):
- **Corpus:** 66 scenarios + arcs valid (`scripts/validate.py`).
- **Analyzer (`scripts/analyze.py`, ~1600 lines):** the **core validity spine** is implemented and threshold-gated on synthetic fixtures — the stated↔revealed **gap** (`compute_gaps`), session aggregates + bootstrap CIs, Bradley–Terry pairwise, card-sort, the **censoring-aware** break-point probe scores, and validity hypotheses **H2–H7** (convergent / discriminant validity, stated–revealed correlation, test–retest of revealed + probes, informant validity).
- **Parity:** the JS on-device projection (`daliu.github.io/patterns-of-choice/runtime/poc-projection.js`) == the Python analyzer (`scripts/check_impl_parity.py`) — `itemScore` agrees on all cases incl. secondary-axis exclusion; the censoring locks hold (`never`-on-$10M scores |8.0|, not the old buggy |5.0|).
- **CI:** `.github/workflows/{analyze,validate}.yml` gate the analyzer + corpus on push/PR.

**What's implemented = the core validity spine (H2–H7), plus H9 self-calibration, H10 cross-situational consistency, H11 moral-circle radius, R2 sacred/protected values, H12 moral hypocrisy / self–other asymmetry, and R1 moral identity centrality (all six partial — see items 1–6 for the deferred discriminants/moderation + JS reveals). What's spec-only = the remaining measurement branches (H8, the rest of the A-series, R3–R6).** That gap is this backlog.

## The build backlog (spec → implementation), priority order
Cheapest-clean and most-machinery-reusing first. Each item: implement scoring in `analyze.py` (+ the JS projection if it touches the on-device reveal) → synthetic fixture(s) with known ground truth → a threshold gate in `check_analyzer_thresholds.py` → keep parity + disciplines → `make check` green → commit/push → journal.

5. **H12 — moral hypocrisy / self–other judgment asymmetry (`scoring.md §18`).** ✅ **BUILT (partial), 2026-06-30.** The measurement primitive (`hypocrisy_asymmetry_by_user` → `H_i = mean_act(severity_other − severity_self)`, the SAME act judged as another's vs one's own on a common 0–10 scale, ≥ 3 matched pairs, §18.1), **H12a** asymmetry reliability (split-half odd/even sessions, `corr(H_i^odd, H_i^even)` lower CI ≥ 0.40, mirroring H10a/H11a exactly), and **H12c** the self-serving directional anchor (`mean_i H_i` lower CI > 0, one-sided — the "holier than thou" signature as a **cohort validity check**, not a per-person verdict) are implemented in `analyze.py` (`--hypocrisy-log`), gated in `check_analyzer_thresholds.py` (H12 threshold expectations + a `check_h12_pairing_lock()` unit-regression — the H12 analog of the §14.1 censoring / `|8.0|` lock, asserting a declined judgment DROPS the pair (never imputed to 0) and the signed delta is preserved so harsher-on-self stays NEGATIVE), on a self-contained synthetic fixture (`analysis/fixtures/sample-hypocrisy-log.json`, 12 participants × 4 sessions × 3 matched probes + one declined judgment, known ground truth). Grounded in Tappin & McKay 2017 / Epley & Dunning 2000 / the actor–observer asymmetry, with Batson's moral hypocrisy as origin; **deliberately avoids** Valdesolo & DeSteno (manipulation-based) and every excluded paradigm. Paired within-person contrast, no new axis — Python-only, parity stays green. `H_i` is N=1 reveal-eligible and value-neutral (harsher-on-others AND harsher-on-self both **described, never ranked** — H12c is walled off as a cohort anchor for exactly this reason); never summed into a composite (§13.5). **Deferred (documented, next increment):** (a) **H12b-discriminant** (R² of `H_i` on `[gap_i, cal_error_i]` < 0.50) — couples to the cohort gap + calibration pipelines, like the H9b/H10b/H11b/R2c discriminants; (b) the **on-device `H_i` reveal** in `poc-projection.js` + its parity lock (Python-only this increment); (c) the **self–other judgment log's real collection + phrasing** (avoid a leading "aren't others worse?") and the **behavioral validation** — does a large `H_i` predict real self–other double standards — via Phase-2 (see "Needs Dave / external").
6. **R1 — moral identity centrality (`scoring.md §19`).** ✅ **BUILT (partial), 2026-06-30.** The measurement primitive (`centrality_facet_by_user` → the **two DISJOINT facets** `internalization_i` and `symbolization_i`, each a within-person mean of its own 1–7 Likert items, ≥ 3 items per facet, §19.1), **R1a** internalization-facet reliability (split-half odd/even sessions, `corr(internalization_i^odd, internalization_i^even)` lower CI ≥ 0.40, mirroring H10a/H11a/H12a exactly), and **R1c** the internalization > symbolization directional anchor (`mean_i(internalization_i − symbolization_i)` lower CI > 0, one-sided — the private-exceeds-public signature Aquino & Reed 2002 report, as a **cohort validity check**, not a per-person verdict) are implemented in `analyze.py` (`--identity-log`), gated in `check_analyzer_thresholds.py` (R1 threshold expectations + `check_r1` rejecting any pooled `centrality`/`moral_identity` key + a `check_r1_no_pool()` unit-regression — the R1 analog of the §14.1 censoring / `|8.0|` lock, asserting the §13.5 facet-separation: the two facets route to DISJOINT means (neither becomes the pooled (I+S)/2), a declined item DROPS from its facet (never imputed 0), and a facet below the ≥3-item floor is SUPPRESSED), on a self-contained synthetic fixture (`analysis/fixtures/sample-identity-centrality-log.json`, 12 participants × 4 sessions × (2 internalization + 2 symbolization) items + one declined item + one counter-case who symbolizes more than they internalize, known ground truth). Grounded in Aquino & Reed 2002 (the canonical, well-replicated instrument); **not** an excluded paradigm. Two-facet within-scale reads, no new axis — Python-only, parity stays green. The facet means are N=1 reveal-eligible and value-neutral (high internalization = integrity OR rigid self-righteousness — the documented *dark side* — never ranked; internalizing not ranked above symbolizing); the two facets are **never pooled** into one moral-identity score (§13.5, the load-bearing discipline here). **Deferred (documented, next increment):** (a) **R1b** — R1's headline **meta-moderation** role (internalization moderating the §6 gap + H10–H12, a negative moderation coefficient) — couples to the cohort gap + H10–H12 pipelines, like the H9b/H10b/H11b/R2c/H12b discriminants; (b) the **on-device facet reveal** in `poc-projection.js` + its parity lock (Python-only this increment); (c) **symbolization-facet reliability** (R1a is anchored on internalization) and (d) the **centrality log's real collection + phrasing** (the Aquino & Reed item stems) — design-gated (see "Needs Dave / external").

## Disciplines every build increment owes (non-negotiable)
- **Unit / never-pool (§13.5):** no forced common scale; the analyzer must not pool across scales (the parity gate already locks secondary-axis exclusion — extend, don't violate).
- **Censoring (§13.2):** `never` cost-of-virtue responses stay right-censored; never made finite (the |8.0| lock is the pattern).
- **No composite score:** never sum a branch's sub-indices into one number; report the facets.
- **Value-neutral / descriptive-only:** the reveal names where you sit; it never ranks. (Extra force for the charged branch, R6.)
- **N=1 interpretability:** the within-person reveal must be defensible for a single user, not just cohort-level.
- **Fraud / non-replication exclusions:** no Stapel / Gino / Ariely-priming / ego-depletion; R7's licensing (if/when built) scoped to the surviving half — no Macbeth cleansing.

## Guardrails for the autonomous build loop
- **Never commit or push a red state.** Run `make check` before every commit; if red, fix or revert — never push broken. (The design loop pushed prose; this loop pushes code to a public repo + a live runtime — the bar is higher.)
- **Keep JS↔Python parity.** Anything touching the on-device reveal changes **both** `analyze.py` and `poc-projection.js`, and `check_impl_parity.py` must stay green.
- **Synthetic data only.** Fixtures with known ground truth; no real human data.
- **Propose, don't lock.** Pre-registration decisions are proposed for Dave's lock, never auto-locked (same as the design loop).
- **One branch per iteration; stop when dry.** If a scan finds no buildable-without-Dave branch left, journal it and halt — don't pad.

## Needs Dave / external (not buildable by the loop — surface, don't guess)
- **IRB + co-PI + real participants** — the whole real-cohort validation.
- **Real κ inter-rater validation** of the language coder (A3 / R3 / R4 / R5) — the loop can build the coder + κ-computation + a synthetic test, but the *real* κ≥0.70 that ungates the language channel needs human raters.
- **REL-2 context-tag inter-rater validity (H10)** — the loop scores cross-situational consistency on synthetic `context:*`-tagged items now (`scoring.md §15`), but tagging the *real* corpus by setting (workplace / family / public / anonymous) and validating that pass (design-doc §3 A1) needs human raters, like the language-coder κ.
- **REL-2 counterparty-distance ordering inter-rater validity (H11)** — the loop scores the moral-circle radius on synthetic `counterparty:*`-tagged items now (`scoring.md §16`) using a **v0.1 DRAFT** ordering map (`analysis/counterparty_distance_map_v0.1.csv`). The specific bin ordering is a **researcher-imposed** value judgment (a CV-2 "smuggled values" risk); validating that the near→far ranking is inter-subjectively agreed (design-doc §3 A1, the REL-2 check) needs human raters. The **§6 Q4 distance/power confound** (excluding `senior`/`subordinate`/`business` and the contrast markers) is already handled in the map; the *ordering of what remains* is what needs the human pass.
- **A2 real-stakes channel** — consequential real stakes (the EV-3/EV-4 keystone) need real money/consequences + IRB; Phase-2, cohort. **This also gates R2's cheap-talk validation** (`scoring.md §17.5`): the loop scores the *professed* protected set `P_i` now, but which `never`s survive a *real* offer (H-A2) needs consequential stakes — so `P_i` is labelled "professed" until then.
- **R2 `taboo`-marker phrasing (Q1)** — R2b's protected≠expensive test uses a light `taboo` field ("was even being asked to price this wrong?", `scoring.md §17.3/§17.5`), scored on synthetic fixtures now. The **exact on-device wording** (avoiding a leading "was this offensive?") and whether it fires after every CoV probe or only the high rungs is a runtime/UX design call — surface to Dave, don't guess.
- **Runtime hosting / stack / multi-user architecture** decisions (`DECISIONS.md §18` left the runtime line open) — propose options, let Dave choose.
