# Weekly Check-in Interview Script — Pilot (Template)

**Status:** Draft script. 30-minute semi-structured interview, conducted weekly during the 4-week pilot (sessions ~7, 14, 21, 28 of the participant's experience). The script alternates a stable common structure with a week-specific question that probes different aspects of the participant's experience as the pilot unfolds.

**Note to researcher:** Read the participant's recent session log before the call so you can reference specific scenarios they've seen. Don't surface scores or interpret their choices — the participant has not seen any profile data and won't until session ~15.

---

## Setting expectations (the first 60 seconds, every week)

> "Hi — thanks for taking the time. This is the weekly check-in for the pilot. About 30 minutes. The conversation is about your experience of the app, not about you — there are no right or wrong answers to anything I ask. You can decline any question without explanation; you can also tell me you'd rather not go into something and I'll move on.
>
> Audio is recording — okay to continue?"

(If declined, proceed without recording; take written notes.)

> "I want to start with anything you've noticed about the app this week — bugs, friction, things that didn't feel right. Then I'll ask about one specific scenario, and then there's a free-form question that changes each week. Last five minutes are logistics for the week ahead."

---

## Section 1: Bug / friction report (5 minutes)

Open-ended, gentle. Goal is to surface usability problems before they compound across sessions.

**Prompts (use as needed):**

- "What's worked well this week?"
- "Anything frustrating? Sessions that didn't load, prompts that confused you, friction at any point?"
- "Have you skipped any sessions? Was there a pattern to which days you skipped?"
- "Was there a scenario this week you wished you could go back and re-do? Why?"
- "Anything about the interface or pacing that felt off?"

**Listening for:**
- Drop-off points (specific session numbers where engagement dipped)
- Item-level confusion (especially in scenarios where the user picked a specific option then says they're not sure what was being asked)
- Modality friction (timer too tight, text too long, branching too complex)
- Mood drift (signs of scrupulosity, anxiety, performative responding)

**Note:** if anything sounds like scrupulosity or active distress, pause the section and use the wellbeing-check language from Section 5 of the exit script. Don't continue the structured interview until the participant is clearly settled.

---

## Section 2: Pick one scenario from this past week (10 minutes)

**Lead-in:**

> "I want to ask about one specific scenario from this past week. From everything you saw — which one most stands out? Doesn't have to be one you're proud of or one you regret. Just one that's stuck with you."

(Wait for them to name it. If they can't, prompt: "What about one where you weren't sure what you'd actually do? Or one that felt unrealistic? Or one that surprised you?")

**Once they pick one:**

- "Walk me through that one again. What was the situation, as you understood it?"
- "What did you choose?"
- "How sure were you of that choice — like, would you have done the same thing in real life?"
- "Has the choice been on your mind since you did it?"
- "If you'd seen that scenario in a different context — say, a friend told you about it instead of the app — would your answer have been the same?"

**Listening for:**
- Whether the user can re-narrate the scenario accurately (interpretability check)
- Whether their stated re-choice matches the recorded choice (consistency / social-desirability signal)
- Whether the scenario felt artificial in a way that would invalidate it as a measure
- Whether the user is forming a meta-model of "what the app is measuring" — if so, flag as a pre-reg threat (gaming)

---

## Section 3: Week-specific question (10 minutes)

The question rotates across the four weeks to probe different aspects of the participant's evolving relationship with the instrument.

### Week 1 (sessions ~7)

> "Do you remember the screen during your first session that said something like — this is a pilot, the instrument isn't validated, you might step away? What did you think when you read that?"

Probes to follow up:
- "Did that change anything about how you approached the app afterward?"
- "Did it make you trust the app more, less, or about the same as you would have without it?"
- "Did anyone you've told about the study react to that framing in a particular way?"

**Listening for:**
- Whether the candor moment is being read as cynicism / defensive lawyering vs. honesty
- Whether it changes engagement (more careful, less careful, equally careful)
- Whether it produces meta-stance ("I'm part of an experiment") that might be its own social-desirability vector

### Week 2 (sessions ~14)

> "Has using this app changed anything about how you notice your daily decisions, if at all? Not in a 'I'm a better person now' way — just whether you've found yourself thinking about choices you wouldn't have thought about before."

Probes:
- "If yes — when did you first notice that?"
- "If no — have you tried to ignore it, or has it just not surfaced?"
- "Has anyone close to you noticed any change?"

**Listening for:**
- Whether the app is producing reflective consciousness about everyday ethics (intended)
- Whether the app is producing self-monitoring anxiety (unintended; scrupulosity adjacent)
- Whether informant-perceptible behavior change is happening (validity-relevant for the Phase-2 informant wave)

### Week 3 (sessions ~21, just before the profile reveal)

> "Tomorrow or the day after — depending on your session cadence — the app is going to show you a profile for the first time. What do you expect to see? What do you hope to see? What are you nervous about?"

Probes:
- "If you had to guess your highest-revealed-virtue-score domain, what would you guess?"
- "Lowest?"
- "Are there any domains you'd rather not see numbers for?"

**Listening for:**
- Whether the user has formed accurate or inaccurate expectations of their profile
- Whether there's anticipatory anxiety about the reveal (signal for how to refine reveal-screen copy)
- Which domains they're most invested in seeing (informs the per-domain priority order in the reveal)

### Week 4 (sessions ~28, after the profile reveal)

> "You've seen the profile now. What did it tell you that you didn't already know?"

Probes:
- "Did anything surprise you?"
- "Did anything feel off — like, this doesn't match how I'd describe myself?"
- "Is there a gap the app surfaced that you'd want to think more about?"
- "Did the profile make you want to keep using the app, stop using it, or stay neutral?"

**Listening for:**
- Surprise content (the highest-signal observation — surprise is where the instrument is informative beyond self-knowledge)
- Mismatches (which read as the app being wrong about the user, vs. the app catching something the user hadn't noticed)
- Forward intention (engagement signal for whether MVP-2 intervention layer would be welcomed)
- Distress signals (any sign that the reveal was unsettling beyond useful)

---

## Section 4: Closing handoff (5 minutes)

> "Logistics for the week ahead. Any scheduling conflicts? Anything you want me to make sure I cover next week? Anything you'd like to skip?"

(Confirm next call time. Confirm participant has a way to reach you for urgent concerns.)

If the wellbeing check earlier surfaced anything: confirm again the participant is okay continuing, or offer to pause / withdraw without compensation penalty.

---

## After the call

Within 4 hours:

1. Mark the recording's anonymization status in the tracker
2. Note any urgent flags (scrupulosity, distress, gaming) in the per-participant log
3. Flag any scenario-level issues for the editorial review queue
4. Update the open-questions-by-week tracker

Within 7 days:

5. Transcribe (auto-transcribed with manual cleanup) and de-identify
6. Delete the raw audio per data-handling policy
