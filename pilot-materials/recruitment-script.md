# Recruitment Script — Pilot (Template)

**Status:** Draft template. Adapted for Prolific format; needs localization for other panels. Co-PI / institutional details are placeholders.

---

## Prolific listing — short form

**Title (max 60 chars):**
> Daily 5-minute ethics-decisions study (4 weeks, $50 max)

**Description (~150 words):**
> We are piloting a new daily-session app that asks you to make small choices in everyday ethical situations — about honesty, fairness, group loyalty, cooperation. The app is research-stage and unvalidated; we want to know whether it works as an instrument, not how you compare to anyone.
>
> Participation: about 5 minutes per day for 4 weeks (you can skip days), plus 4 short weekly check-in calls (30 minutes each) and one exit interview (45 minutes).
>
> Compensation: up to $50 total, prorated by sessions completed. Full $50 requires ≥14 sessions and the exit interview.
>
> What you get: nothing material beyond the compensation. We will not show you scores; we are not coaching you; this is not therapy. You will help us figure out whether a research instrument works, and your time is paid for.
>
> Withdraw anytime, no penalty. Your data is deleted within 90 days of study end.

## Eligibility pre-screening

To proceed, participants confirm:

- Age 18+
- Fluent in English (the pilot is English-only)
- Have used a daily-cadence app before (so familiarity with the format isn't a confound)
- Have access to a reliable device (phone or laptop) for the 4-week duration
- Available for 4 × 30-min check-in calls + 1 × 45-min exit interview

Pre-screening exclusions:

- Currently in crisis or active mental-health treatment (the study is not therapy; we don't want to crowd out the care you have)
- Diagnosed scrupulosity / OCD with active symptoms (constant moral self-monitoring is an exacerbating factor we don't want to add to)
- Professional moral psychologist or UX researcher (we want participants close to the eventual user demographic, not study-aware peers)

Pre-screening is opt-in disclosure on the listing; no medical records collected.

---

## Composition target (not enforced, just sought)

Of the 10 participants we recruit:

- ~3 confident technical users (comfortable with daily-cadence apps, knowledge work)
- ~4 mid-comfort knowledge workers (use apps but don't seek them out)
- ~3 less app-comfortable users (use phones for basic communication, less likely to engage with novel app interfaces)

Demographic targets:
- Mixed ages (roughly evenly distributed across 20s, 30s, 40s+, with at least 2 over 50)
- Mixed genders
- Mixed cultural backgrounds (recognizing the WEIRD-bias concern flagged in concept.md; the pilot is too small to test cross-cultural validity but we can avoid maximal WEIRD over-representation)

Composition is sought, not enforced — we will not exclude qualifying participants who don't fit a target slot.

## After acceptance

Successful applicants receive:

1. **Welcome email** (within 48 hours of accept) with:
   - Confirmation of compensation structure
   - Link to the consent form (`consent-form.md` derived)
   - Calendar link to schedule the first weekly check-in
   - Link to the daily-session app and onboarding instructions

2. **Onboarding instructions** with:
   - Account creation (passkey or magic-link)
   - First-session expectations (~10 min — longer than typical sessions due to the values inventory)
   - The "candor moment" copy that names the unvalidated nature of the instrument (see `../onboarding.md`)

3. **Weekly check-in calls** (Saturdays or as scheduled) for 4 weeks. Voice call only; no video required; recording is optional and consented to separately per call.

4. **Exit interview** (week 5) — 45-min structured interview. Recording optional. Transcript anonymized; raw audio deleted within 30 days.

---

## What we DON'T do in recruitment

- We do not advertise the project as "self-improvement," "becoming a better person," or anything similar. The candor framing is consistent across recruitment, onboarding, and the instrument itself.
- We do not promise insights. We do not promise that participation will be transformative, illuminating, or worth-it-beyond-the-money.
- We do not pre-prime participants on the domain taxonomy. The recruitment listing names the broad area ("everyday ethical situations") but does not enumerate the four MVP-1 domains or any of the specific scenario types.
- We do not use language that primes scrupulosity or moral perfectionism (no "become more honest" / "improve your character" / similar).
- We do not share participant identities with each other, with informants without consent, or with anyone outside the small research team.

---

## Open recruitment questions

- **Platform choice**: Prolific is the working assumption (well-established for academic-ish behavioral studies, good demographic diversity, fair compensation norms). Alternatives worth considering: CloudResearch, mTurk Prime, locally-sourced via co-PI's institution. The decision affects pre-screening capabilities and compensation conventions.
- **Pre-recruitment screening interview**: should we add a 15-min screening call before final acceptance, to confirm fit and surface any concerns the listing didn't address? Would tighten quality but adds friction.
- **Public posting vs invitation-only**: the small (n=10) target makes invitation-only via the co-PI's network practical; would surface a different demographic mix than open Prolific recruitment.

These choices are deferred to the moment of actual pilot launch.
