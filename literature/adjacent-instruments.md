# Adjacent Instruments and Products

What already exists in this space. Mostly to inform competitive positioning and to identify what patterns-of-choice is and is not.

## Research instruments (not products)

- **Moral Foundations Questionnaire (MFQ-30 / MFQ-2)** — Haidt and colleagues. Survey-based, self-report. Captures stated weighting on five (or six, MFQ-2) foundations. Not behavioral; not longitudinal. Free at moralfoundations.org.
- **HEXACO-100, HEXACO-60** — Lee & Ashton. Trait personality with the Honesty-Humility factor. Free at hexaco.org. The closest existing instrument to patterns-of-choice's measurement-layer construct.
- **Schwartz PVQ-RR (2022)** — 57-item portrait questionnaire across 19 values, validated in 49 cultures.
- **Values in Action Survey (VIA Survey)** — viacharacter.org, the consumer-facing front for VIA-IS. Free version available. Owned by the VIA Institute on Character. The closest existing "consumer" version of a values measurement. Notably: it is a *one-shot* survey with no longitudinal-tracking architecture.
- **Defining Issues Test (DIT-2)** — Rest et al. The post-Kohlberg measure of moral reasoning. Recognition-based; argued to be more developmentally informative than the original Moral Judgment Interview.
- **Moral Identity Scale (Aquino & Reed 2002)** — captures self-importance of moral traits. Short (10 items). Predicts some prosocial behavior.

## Consumer products in the values / character space

- **VIA Survey (free + paid reports)** — closest consumer-facing comparison. Strengths: free, brief, validated psychometrics. Weaknesses: one-shot survey, not behavioral, no longitudinal architecture, no gap analysis. Patterns of Choice's positioning is partly *what VIA could have been if designed in 2026 with longitudinal behavioral measurement.*
- **Authentic Happiness / Penn questionnaires (Seligman lab)** — a portal of brief questionnaires including the VIA. Largely an artifact of the early-2000s positive-psychology web.
- **Big Five / Myers-Briggs / Enneagram apps** — distinct space; personality typology, not ethics. Includes 16personalities.com, Truity, etc. Patterns of Choice should explicitly *not* be associated with this typology-driven framing.
- **Sano (Pavlok-style)** — behavior-change apps targeting habits. Not values-based.
- **BetterUp, Headspace, Calm, Waking Up** — adjacent products in the contemplative-practice space. Closest in *form factor* (daily practice, subscription) but distant in *content* (mindfulness/meditation, not ethics). Waking Up (Sam Harris) is closest in voice — secular contemplative, philosophy-adjacent, single-author.
- **Roam / Daylio / Stoic** — journaling apps. Daylio uses categorical mood tracking. Stoic offers daily prompts and pre-set values reflections. Stoic is the closest in *spirit* — values-anchored, journaling-based — but is self-report-only and offers no measurement architecture.

## Academic-grade adjacent products

- **PsyToolkit / Lab.js / Inquisit** — open-source platforms for delivering behavioral psychology experiments. Patterns of Choice's measurement layer should probably *use* one of these rather than build from scratch for the validation studies.
- **Connecticut Center for Wisdom Studies / Berlin Wisdom Paradigm** (Baltes, Staudinger) — wisdom-rated open-ended responses. Methodologically interesting but expensive (requires expert raters).

## "Character education" school programs (mostly unvalidated)

- **Character Lab (Angela Duckworth's nonprofit, characterlab.org)** — collision with the project's original working name. Duckworth's organization provides "playbooks" for educators based on research summaries; not an instrument, more a content / advocacy organization. Collision resolved by renaming the project to *patterns-of-choice*. Worth keeping aware of for future positioning purposes.
- **KIPP character framework, Knowledge Is Power Program** — based partly on Peterson & Seligman's strengths. Not validated.
- **CASEL Social and Emotional Learning framework** — broader than character; uses informant-report instruments validated by IES.

## What this tells us about positioning

- **No existing product systematically operationalizes the revealed/stated gap.** The concept doc's claim that "none systematically operationalize the gap" appears to be correct after this scan. This is the genuine wedge.
- **VIA is the most direct comparison consumer-facing.** Patterns of Choice should be ready to articulate the difference (behavioral vs. self-report; longitudinal vs. one-shot; gap-analysis vs. profile).
- **Stoic and Waking Up are the closest in market position.** Both are subscription-based contemplative-practice apps with a single-author voice. Both have demonstrated subscription-based business models can work in this space without ads/social comparison/engagement-farming. Useful precedent.
- **The Duckworth name collision was real and resolved by renaming.** "Character Lab" already exists as Duckworth's organization, which is why this project is now *patterns-of-choice*. For future positioning, retaining awareness of Duckworth's brand-adjacency matters.
