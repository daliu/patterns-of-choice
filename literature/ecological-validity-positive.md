# Ecological Validity: What DOES Bridge Lab to Life

Companion to `ecological-validity.md` (the negative case: Bostyn 2018, FeldmanHall 2012 — hypothetical trolleys/dictators fail). This pass is the positive direction: which low-stakes paradigms *do* track real moral behavior, and what that implies for MVP-1.

Headline: ecological validity is not uniform. Honesty under monetary incentive (die-roll, sender-receiver), trust-game trustworthiness, and HEXACO honesty-humility all show meaningful lab–life correspondence. Construct matters more than methodology label.

---

## 1. Honesty under monetary stakes — the cleanest positive case

**Abeler, Nosenzo, & Raymond (2019)** *Preferences for truth-telling*, Econometrica 87, 1115–1153. Meta-analysis of 429 treatments / 90 papers / N≈44,000 across 47 countries on Fischbacher-style die-roll dishonesty. Average standardized report = 0.234; participants forgo ~75% of monetary gain from lying. Aversion robust across stake sizes and cultures. Two motivations dominate: preference for *being* honest and for being *seen* as honest.

**Fischbacher & Föllmi-Heusi (2013)** *Lies in disguise*, JEEA 11, 525–547. Original paradigm. **Gerlach, Teodorescu, & Hertwig (2019)** *The truth about lies*, Psychological Bulletin 145, 1–44, confirms Abeler across variants.

**Cohn, Maréchal, Tannenbaum, & Zünd (2019)** *Civic honesty around the globe*, Science 365, 70–73. 17,303 "lost" wallets in 355 cities / 40 countries. Return rates: 46% empty, 61% $13.45, 72% $94.15 — more money, *more* likely returned, contrary to both layperson and economist predictions.

**Dai, Galeotti, & Villeval (2017)** *Cheating in the lab predicts fraud in the field*, Management Science 64, 1081–1100. Real fare-evaders vs. fare-payers from Lyon transit; lab die-roll cheating discriminated the groups. Closest available individual-level lab→field test for honesty. **Cohn, Maréchal, & Noll (2015)** *Bad boys*, ReStud — lab coin-flip cheating predicted adolescent school misconduct.

**Implication for MVP-1's truth-telling domain.** Of the four chosen domains, truth-telling has by far the strongest lab–life evidence. Die-roll / sender-receiver paradigms are operationally cheap, web-administrable, with meta-anchored base rates. **Do not** import Mazar/Ariely 2008's *moral priming* framing — failed in a 25-lab RRR (Verschuere et al. 2018, N=5,786); Ariely's program is partially compromised by the Gino fraud case. The matrix task as a behavior elicitor is fine; the priming claim is not.

## 2. Dictator, trust, and cooperation games — mixed, with individual-level signal

Aggregate news is sobering. **Galizzi & Navarro-Martinez (2019)** *Management Science* systematically tested dictator/UG/PD/trust games against real-world social behaviors. Only 39.7% of lab–field correlations significant; mean r = 0.14. Single-game predictive validity is poor.

Individual-level findings are more nuanced:

**Karlan (2005)** *Using experimental economics to measure social capital*, AER 95, 1688–1699. Peruvian microfinance group members: trust-game *trustworthiness* (amount returned) predicted real loan repayment over the next year. Trust-game *trust* (amount sent) did not. Trustworthiness is recoverable; "trust" is diffuse.

**Franzen & Pointner (2013)** *External validity of giving in the dictator game*, Experimental Economics 16, 155–169. Misdirected-letter technique: lab dictator generosity predicted return of cash-containing "misdirected" letters weeks-to-years post-lab, across two studies.

**Benz & Meier (2008)** *Do people behave in experiments as in the field?* Experimental Economics 11, 268–281. Same subjects' lab dictator giving vs. actual charity donations across 8 semesters. Lab and field giving correlate within-person; lab over-states field.

**Peysakhovich, Nowak, & Rand (2014)** *Humans display a cooperative phenotype*, Nat Commun 5, 4939. Behavior across dictator/trust/UG/PD/PG loads on a single cooperative-phenotype factor; test–retest r ≈ 0.4–0.6 across paradigms. The lab measures *something* about cooperation that travels across games.

**Implication.** Three of four MVP-1 domains rely on paradigms with individual-level lab–field validity in r ≈ 0.10–0.30. The response is not "drop them" but "do not over-claim from any single scenario." Aggregating ~12 scenarios × ~7 sessions per domain is what Peysakhovich et al. justify. Pre-register effects at the aggregated-domain level, not single-scenario.

## 3. HEXACO Honesty-Humility — the strongest validity case for any moral-construct measure

Strongest evidence in the literature that *any* personality measure predicts real moral behavior — precisely the construct MVP-1 uses as its convergent-validity hook.

**Pletzer, Bentvelzen, Oostrom, & de Vries (2019)** *Personality and workplace deviance: Big Five vs. HEXACO*, J. Vocational Behavior 112, 369–383. H ρ = −0.48 with workplace deviance — the strongest single trait–behavior correlation in the meta-analysis. HEXACO explains ~32% of variance vs. ~19% for Big Five; H's incremental validity over Big Five is large.

**Lee, Ashton, Morrison, Cordery, & Dunlop (2008)** *Predicting workplace delinquency and integrity with HEXACO and FFM*, Human Performance 21, 145–168. H predicted theft, vandalism, absenteeism, Employee Integrity Index after controlling for Big Five. *Fairness* facet was the strongest single predictor — directly matching MVP-1's truth-telling and resource-allocation domains.

**Hilbig, Zettler, & colleagues** (multiple, 2012–2018). H is the primary trait predictor of dictator-game giving (ρ ≈ 0.20–0.30, replicated dozens of times). H differentiates *active* cooperation (non-exploitation in DG) from *reactive* cooperation (non-retaliation in UG, which loads on Agreeableness). Directly testable in MVP-1.

**Thielmann, Spadaro, & Balliet (2020)** *Personality and prosocial behavior: a theoretical framework and meta-analysis*, Psychological Bulletin 146, 30–90. 770 studies, 3,523 effects, 6 economic games. Meta-analytic ρ̂ ≈ 0.20 between H and dictator giving / PD cooperation. Among the largest robust trait–behavior relationships in the field.

**Zettler, Thielmann, Hilbig, & Moshagen (2020)** *Nomological net of HEXACO*, PPS 15, 723–760. H's correlates densely consistent with its theoretical definition (fairness, sincerity, modesty, greed avoidance).

**Implication.** MVP-1 pre-registers `r ≥ 0.30` between revealed truth-telling and HEXACO H. Given meta-analytic ρs ~0.20–0.30, this is **at the upper edge** of empirical expectation. Drop to `r ≥ 0.25` to keep the pre-registration defensible; `r ≥ 0.35` would be a strong positive result.

## 4. Experience sampling — what daily moral life actually looks like

**Hofmann, Wisneski, Brandt, & Skitka (2014)** *Morality in everyday life*, Science 345, 1340–1343. EMA of N=1,252 adults, 5 signals/day × 3 days, 13,240 morally relevant events. Everyday morality is *mundane* (gossip, fairness, small-context generosity), not dramatic. Evidence for both moral contagion and moral licensing within daily life.

**Bollich, Doris, Vazire, Raison, Jackson, & Mehl (2016)** *Eavesdropping on character: Assessing everyday moral behaviors*, JRP 61, 15–21. Electronically Activated Recorder (EAR) across 3 samples (combined N=186), coding ambient audio for moral behaviors. *Stable individual differences in moral behavior are observable in daily life*, with stability across weekends comparable to neutral linguistic behaviors. Strongest evidence that moral *traits* aren't just self-report fictions.

**Implication.** Hofmann anchors the choice of mundane over dramatic scenarios (already in concept doc). Bollich 2016 means moral behavior has stable individual variation across short time windows; MVP-1's test–retest target of r ≥ 0.60 (weeks 1–2 vs. 3–4) is empirically defensible. ESM-style daily check-ins are deferred to MVP-2; within-session scenario behavior is sufficient mini-ESM for Phase-1.

## 5. Informant reports — operational guidance

For MVP-1's opt-in week-4 informant wave: how many, which relationships, asked what?

**Vazire (2010)** *Self–Other Knowledge Asymmetry (SOKA) model*, JPSP 98, 281–300. Informants out-predict self on traits high in evaluativeness *and* observability. Honesty-humility is the prototypically evaluative-and-observable trait — exactly where informant > self.

**Connelly & Ones (2010)** *An other perspective on personality*, Psychological Bulletin 136, 1092–1122. 263 samples, N=44,178. Three findings matter: (a) multi-informant ratings outperform self-report for predicting behavior in honesty, conscientiousness, agreeableness; (b) *interpersonal intimacy* of the informant matters more than acquaintance length; (c) inter-rater reliability rises rapidly from 1 to 2 informants and slows after 3 — marginal validity gain from #3 → #4 is small.

**Funder (2012)** *Accurate personality judgment*, CDPS 21, 177–182. Confirms close-other > stranger > self for evaluative traits.

**Vazire & Carlson (2010, 2011).** Informant reports predict job performance, GPA, health at validity matching or exceeding self-reports; close-other > stranger ratings.

**Implication for MVP-1 informant wave.** Three operational decisions:

- **How many?** 2 minimum, 3 ideal, 4 unnecessary. Reliability curve plateaus near 3.
- **Which?** Close-others in honesty-relevant contexts. Romantic partner + coworker is the canonical pair; casual friends weaker; strangers/distant relatives are noise.
- **Asked what?** HEXACO-60 informant form (validated) + 3–5 incident-level behavior items per domain (e.g., "When this person tells you something inconvenient for them, how often do you find out later they were not being fully honest?"). Incident-level items outperform abstract trait items.

MVP-1 pre-registers `informant H r ≥ 0.25 with revealed truth-telling`. Defensible with ≥2 informants per target; with only 1, attenuate to `r ≥ 0.20`.

## 6. Direct implications for MVP-1 design

Numbered, in order of empirical strength.

1. **Use die-roll-style and sender-receiver paradigms in the truth-telling domain.** Abeler-meta-anchored base rates; strongest individual-level lab→field evidence (Dai 2017; Cohn 2015, 2019). Do *not* import the Mazar/Ariely 2008 *moral priming* framing — failed in 25-lab RRR. Matrix task as behavior elicitor is fine.

2. **Recruit ≥2 informants per user; aim for 3; do not pursue 4.** Reliability gain plateaus at 3 (Connelly & Ones 2010). Romantic partner + coworker canonical.

3. **Drop the pre-registered revealed–HEXACO target from 0.30 to 0.25.** Above the upper edge of meta-analytic expectation given Thielmann 2020 (ρ̂ ≈ 0.20) and Pletzer 2019.

4. **Aggregate across scenarios per domain; do not rely on any single scenario.** Galizzi & Navarro-Martinez 2019 mean per-game lab–field r = 0.14; Peysakhovich 2014 cross-game phenotype is moderately stable. Pre-register effects at the aggregated-domain level.

5. **Add one real-stakes channel if budget allows.** Cohn 2019, Karlan 2005, Dai 2017 all involve real money. One $5–$10 charity-allocation per user would substantially strengthen the ecological-validity claim (`mvp.md` Q4).

6. **Score HEXACO-60 informant form at H facet level, not just domain.** Facet-level prediction often exceeds domain-level for specific behaviors (Pletzer 2019; Lee–Ashton). Domain H + 4 facets (fairness, sincerity, modesty, greed avoidance) is the right convergent-validity structure.

7. **Defer ESM-style daily check-ins to MVP-2.** Bollich 2016 and Hofmann 2014 support the value, but operational burden and engagement-vs-behavior confound exceed validation gain at n=200.

## 7. What we don't know

- **Web-based, gamified-feeling administration of die-roll/dictator paradigms.** Entire honesty literature is in-person or lab-mediated MTurk/Prolific. Whether a *narratively-embedded, daily-puzzle-framed* version preserves lab→field signal is open — this is the MVP-1 design hypothesis; no direct precedent.

- **Cost-of-virtue probe individual-level reliability.** Crockett et al. 2014 PNAS established the harm-magnitude paradigm at group level. Within-person stake-laddering test–retest is not literature-anchored at the level MVP-1 needs (r ≥ 0.50). The target is reasonable but exploratory.

- **Four-domain factor structure recovery.** Closest analog is Peysakhovich cooperative-phenotype, which found cross-game *generality*, not domain-specific factors. MVP-1 CFA target (loadings > 0.5 on intended factor) may be optimistic; pre-register alternative single-factor model too.

- **In-group/out-group individual-level lab–life correspondence.** Parochial empathy (Cikara/Bruneau) measurable in lab; individual-level lab–life validity less established than honesty/cooperation. Weakest MVP-1 domain for ecological validity; flag as exploratory.

- **Informant response rates for cold-emailed contacts of Prolific-recruited participants.** Operational unknown.

- **Order/contextual effects across the 8-week 4-domain rotation.** Order effects exist for abstract moral judgment (Schwitzgebel et al.) but not for repeated behavioral scenarios. Counterbalance the rotation seed at enrollment; pre-register as control.

---

*Highest-leverage move: anchor every pre-registered correlation in the relevant meta-analysis (Abeler 2019, Pletzer 2019, Thielmann 2020, Connelly & Ones 2010), not aspirational round numbers. `r ≥ 0.30` for revealed–HEXACO is the one to lower.*
